Property Investment Scams

Fake Pre-Launch Sales & Too-Good-To-Be-True Deals

üö® CRITICAL WARNING

Property investment scams advertise non-existent properties or fake pre-launch opportunities with guaranteed returns. These scams target people's desire to invest in real estate, using high-pressure tactics and promises of below-market prices to steal booking fees or larger deposits.

üö© Major Red Flags

üí∞ Guaranteed Returns

Claims of "guaranteed 25% ROI" or "guaranteed rental income." It's ILLEGAL in Singapore for developers to guarantee returns. Real estate has market risks.

üìâ Below Market Price

"20% below market value" in prime locations like District 1. Real prime properties don't sell significantly below market rates.

‚è∞ Extreme Urgency

Countdown timers, "only 8 units left," "offer expires in 4 hours." Legitimate property sales don't use such high-pressure tactics.

üí≥ Immediate Payment

Requests for booking fees via PayNow or bank transfer before viewing property or signing Option to Purchase.

üìÑ No URA Approval

Can't provide URA development approval number or project details aren't on URA website.

üë§ No Licensed Agent

Direct contact without licensed property agent. Real developers work through CEA-registered agents.

üîó External Email Offers

Unsolicited emails about "exclusive opportunities." Real developers advertise through official channels.

üèóÔ∏è Vague Project Details

No specific developer name, project location, or TOP (Temporary Occupation Permit) date.

‚úÖ How to Protect Yourself

1. Verify Developer Credentials

Check developer company registration with ACRA and URA. Established developers have track records and completed projects.

2. Check URA Website

All legitimate property developments must have URA approval. Verify project details at www.ura.gov.sg.

3. Use Licensed Property Agents

Work only with CEA-registered agents. Check agent license at www.cea.gov.sg. Licensed agents are accountable and insured.

4. Never Pay Without OTP

Don't pay booking fees without signed Option to Purchase prepared by lawyer. Legitimate transactions require proper legal documentation.

5. Conduct Due Diligence

Engage a lawyer to review all documents before any payment. Check for caveats, charges, and developer's financial health.

6. Visit Show Flat/Site

Insist on viewing the show flat or project site. Scammers often make excuses about "pre-launch" unavailability.

7. Research Market Prices

Compare prices on PropertyGuru, 99.co, and recent URA transaction data. If it's too cheap, it's suspicious.

8. Verify "Guaranteed Returns"

Guaranteed returns are illegal in Singapore. Any such promise is automatically a scam.

9. Check Developer Website

Visit the developer's official website directly. Real projects are prominently featured with full details.

10. Report Suspicious Offers

Report to CEA, URA, and Singapore Police Force. This helps protect other potential victims.

üìã Legitimate Property Purchase Process

1. Research developer and project (URA records, past projects)
2. Engage licensed property agent
3. View show flat and project site
4. Engage lawyer to review sale documentation
5. Sign Option to Purchase (OTP) prepared by lawyer
6. Pay booking fee (typically 1-5% of purchase price)
7. Exercise option within timeframe
8. Complete sale and purchase agreement
9. Make downpayment and arrange financing
10. Legal completion and handover

üí° Remember

Real estate is Singapore's biggest investment for most people. Never rush into property purchases based on unsolicited emails or high-pressure sales tactics. Take time to verify every detail, engage professionals (lawyer and licensed agent), and remember: if a prime property deal seems too good to be true, it's almost certainly a scam.
