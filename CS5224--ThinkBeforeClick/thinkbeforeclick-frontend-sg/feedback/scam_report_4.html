Prize scams notify victims that they've "won" a contest, lottery, or giveaway they never entered. These scams exploit the excitement of winning to trick victims into paying "processing fees" or revealing personal information. The core principle: if you didn't enter, you can't win.

‚úÖ How to Protect Yourself

üõ°Ô∏è Stay Safe from Prize Scams

1. Verify Through Official Channels

Visit the company's official website or contact their customer service directly using verified contact information. Don't use numbers or links provided in the "winner" notification.

2. Never Pay to Claim Prizes

Legitimate prizes are free. Any request for payment, no matter how small, is a scam. This includes "processing fees," "taxes," "shipping," or "verification charges."

3. Research the Contest

Search for the contest name online. Real contests have official terms and conditions, announcement posts, and are widely publicized.

4. Check Official Social Media

Look for winner announcements on the company's verified social media accounts (look for the blue checkmark).

5. Don't Share Personal Information

Never provide banking details, OTP codes, passwords, or NRIC/passport numbers to claim a prize. Winners are verified through official registration data.

6. Be Skeptical of Urgency

Real companies give winners reasonable time to claim prizes. Countdown timers and "claim now or lose it" messages are pressure tactics.

7. Ignore Unsolicited Messages

If you receive a prize notification you weren't expecting, assume it's a scam until proven otherwise through independent verification.

8. Report Suspicious Messages

Report scams to the company being impersonated, Singapore Police Force, and ScamShield app to help protect others.

The excitement of "winning" can cloud judgment. Always pause, verify independently, and remember: legitimate companies don't need your money to give you a prize. If it requires payment or feels rushed, it's almost certainly a scam. When in doubt, walk away - no real prize is worth the risk of financial loss or identity theft.
