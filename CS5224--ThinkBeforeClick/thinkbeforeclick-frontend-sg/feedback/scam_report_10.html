Online Marketplace Scams

Fake Buyer Payment & Seller Fraud

üö® HIGH RISK: Seller Beware

Marketplace scams target sellers with fake payment confirmations, claiming money has been transferred when it hasn't. Scammers create elaborate fake "buyer protection" or "escrow service" emails to trick sellers into shipping items before receiving real payment. This scam costs sellers both their items AND money if they fall for additional payment requests.

üé≠ How Marketplace Scams Work

üì± Typical Scam Scenario

1
You list an item - iPhone, laptop, or any valuable item on Carousell, Facebook Marketplace, etc.
2
"Buyer" contacts you - Seems interested, asks basic questions, agrees to your price quickly
3

4

5

6
You lose everything - If you ship the item or pay fees, you lose your item, money, and get nothing in return

üö© Common Scam Tactics

üí≥ Fake "Protection" Services

Claims payment is held by "Carousell Protection," "Buyer Protection," or fake escrow services. These services don't exist on peer-to-peer platforms.

üìß Official-Looking Emails

Sends fake confirmation emails that look more "official" than real platform notifications, complete with logos and professional formatting.

üí∞ "Release Fee" Requests

Claims you need to pay a small fee, tax, or verification charge to "release" the payment. Real platforms never ask sellers to pay to receive money.

‚è∞ Urgency Tactics

"Confirm within 24 hours or transaction will be cancelled." Pressures you to act before verifying the payment.

üì± Out-of-Platform Communication

Moves conversation to WhatsApp, Telegram, or email where platform can't monitor or intervene.

üéØ Overpayment Scam

Claims to have "accidentally overpaid" and asks you to refund the difference before the original payment (which never existed) clears.

üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Yourself

1. Verify Payment in YOUR Bank

Open your banking app and check if money has actually arrived in your account. Don't trust screenshots, emails, or "payment confirmations" from buyers.

2. Prefer Face-to-Face Cash

Meet in public places (malls, MRT stations) for cash transactions. Cash is immediate, verifiable, and irreversible.

3. No Escrow on Peer Platforms

Carousell, Facebook Marketplace, and similar platforms don't hold payments. Any claim of "held funds" is a scam.

4. Bank Transfer Verification

If accepting bank transfers, wait until the money appears in YOUR account before handing over the item. Don't accept screenshots as "proof."

5. Never Pay to Receive Payment

Legitimate transactions don't require you to pay fees, taxes, or verification charges to receive your money. This is always a scam.

6. Ignore Official-Looking Emails

Scammers send fake emails. Verify payment only through your bank app, never through emails from buyers.

7. Stay On Platform Chat

Keep all communication within the platform's chat system. This provides evidence if disputes arise and allows platform moderation.

8. Be Suspicious of Quick Agreement

Scammers agree to prices immediately without negotiation and may offer to pay extra for "rush" or "shipping." This is a red flag.

9. No Shipping Without Payment

Never ship items before receiving confirmed payment in your bank account. Once shipped, you have zero recourse.

10. Trust Your Instincts

If something feels off - overly eager buyer, complicated payment process, urgency - walk away. Better to lose a sale than lose your item.
