‚úì How Real CPF Actually Communicates

Official CPF Board Practices:

  • All account management done through official CPF website (cpf.gov.sg)
  • Important notices sent via registered mail to your home address
  • Never asks for Singpass password or OTP via email/SMS
  • No "mandatory updates" required via email links
  • Updates are communicated through CPF mobile app or official portal
  • Account issues require in-person verification at CPF Service Centre
  • Customer service only at official hotline: 1800 227 1188
  • Emails only from @cpf.gov.sg domain (verify carefully)

üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Your CPF Savings

1. Access CPF Directly Only

Always type www.cpf.gov.sg directly into your browser. Never click links in emails or SMS claiming to be from CPF. Bookmark the official site for easy access.

2. Verify Through Official Channels

If you receive a suspicious CPF message, call the official hotline at 1800 227 1188 (from cpf.gov.sg website, not from the message). CPF staff can confirm if any action is needed.

3. Never Share Singpass Credentials

Your Singpass password and OTP are personal. CPF will NEVER ask for these. Anyone asking for your Singpass details is a scammer, no exceptions.

4. Enable 2FA on Singpass

Use Singpass Face Verification or biometric authentication. This adds a layer of protection even if scammers get your password.

5. Check Sender Email Carefully

Hover over sender addresses to verify. Real CPF emails come from @cpf.gov.sg. Scammers use similar-looking domains like @cpf-board.com or @cpf.com.sg.

6. Ignore Urgent Deadlines

CPF doesn't suspend accounts via email. Real account issues are communicated through multiple official channels over time, not with sudden deadlines.

7. Use Official CPF Mobile App

Download the official CPF mobile app for account access. Check all notifications within the app, not from external messages.

8. Educate Family Members

Older family members are often targeted. Teach them to verify any CPF communication with you or by calling the official hotline before taking action.

9. Report Suspicious Messages

Forward scam emails to phishing@cpf.gov.sg and report to Singapore Police Force. This helps protect other CPF members.

10. Regular Account Monitoring

Check your CPF account regularly through the official portal. Early detection of suspicious activity can prevent major losses.
