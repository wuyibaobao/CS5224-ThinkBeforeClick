üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Yourself

1. Real Fines Come by Mail

Legitimate traffic fines are always sent via registered mail to your registered address. LTA does not issue fines only through SMS or email.

2. Check OneMotoring Portal

Log into www.OneMotoring.com.sg directly (don't click links). All real outstanding fines appear in your dashboard with full details and photo evidence.

3. Verify Before Paying

If you receive a suspicious fine notice, call LTA hotline at 1800 225 5582. They can confirm whether you have any outstanding fines.

4. Never Click SMS Links

Don't click payment links in SMS or emails. Always access OneMotoring directly by typing the URL yourself or using the official mobile app.

5. Check Physical Mail

If you haven't received physical mail, the SMS/email is likely a scam. Real traffic fines require registered mail notification.

6. Look for Specific Details

Real fines include your full name, complete NRIC, full vehicle number, and precise violation details. Generic information indicates a scam.

7. Verify Payment Methods

Official payment is ONLY through OneMotoring portal or AXS stations. Any other payment method is a scam.

8. Be Skeptical of Threats

Real enforcement doesn't threaten immediate arrest or vehicle seizure via SMS. These are scare tactics used by scammers.

9. Check Sender Details

Real LTA messages come from official shortcodes or @lta.gov.sg emails. Verify sender addresses carefully.

10. Report Scam Messages

Forward suspicious messages to LTA and report to Singapore Police Force through ScamShield app.

üéØ Why These Scams Are Effective

1. Fear Factor: Nobody wants to face legal consequences or higher fines
2. Common Occurrence: Many people have committed minor traffic violations
3. Looks Official: Messages use government terminology and formatting
4. Small Amounts: $150-200 fines seem reasonable and worth paying
5. Urgency: Threats of escalation pressure immediate action
6. Convenience: Paying online seems easier than verifying

üí° Golden Rule

If you didn't receive physical mail about a traffic fine, any SMS or email claiming you have a fine is almost certainly a scam. LTA requires registered mail for all traffic violations. When in doubt, check OneMotoring portal directly or call the official LTA hotline - never click links in suspicious messages.
