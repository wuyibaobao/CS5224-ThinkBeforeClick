WARNING: University Tuition Scam!

You almost clicked on a fake NUS tuition refund notification - a common scam targeting students!

üö© Red Flags in This Scam:

  • Unexpected Refund: Universities rarely have tuition "overpayments" due to automated billing systems
  • Email Notification: Real NUS financial notices are sent via official Student Portal, not external emails
  • External Links: Legitimate refunds are processed through NUS Student Portal only
  • Requests Banking Info: NUS already has your bank details from initial enrollment
  • Generic Student ID: Real notices include your full name and complete student ID
  • Forfeiture Threat: Creates false urgency with "unclaimed refunds will be forfeited"
  • Domain Check: Real NUS emails come from @nus.edu.sg or @u.nus.edu domains
  • MOE Grant Excuse: Tuition Grant adjustments are communicated through official channels

‚úÖ How to Verify University Refunds:

1. Log in directly to NUS Student Portal to check your account status
2. Contact Office of Financial Services at 6516 1234 to verify any refund claims
3. Real refunds appear in your Student Portal financial statements
4. NUS will NEVER ask for bank details via email - they already have it
5. Check email sender address carefully - must be @nus.edu.sg
6. Visit Office of Financial Services in person if uncertain
7. Report phishing emails to NUS IT Security at itcare@nus.edu.sg
8. Never click links in unexpected "refund" emails
